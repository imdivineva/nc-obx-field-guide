A VIRTUAL WINDSHIELD JOURNEY

North Carolina's coast, the way you'd actually meet it — from a car window.

Not the seven natural zones, arranged like an encyclopedia. Two literal road trips, narrated like a documentary tour guide. At every stop, the question is the same: what can you see from the road right here, and what does that tell you about where you are?

01
Episode 01 · Durham to Nags Head · US-64 East

Watch North Carolina Become Coastal

Begin among the hills and red clay of the Piedmont. Follow the land as it flattens, darkens, floods, opens into sounds, and finally narrows into the Outer Banks.

Episode 02 · Corolla to Ocracoke · NC-12 South

Travel the Length of the Banks

Follow the barrier islands through maritime forests, beach towns, dunes, marshes, inlets, capes, storm-shaped islands, and a ferry crossing to Ocracoke.

Someone driving from Raleigh can follow Episode 01 in real time, stop by stop, all the way to the beach. Someone already staying in Nags Head can skip straight to Episode 02 and start reading the islands they're standing on.

Episode 01 covers how you enter the coastal system — inland Piedmont, through the Coastal Plain, into wetlands, an estuary, two sounds, and finally a barrier island. Episode 02 covers how to read the coastal system once you're inside it — how the islands themselves vary in width, development, vegetation, exposure, and orientation as you travel their length.
