a virtual windshield journey

North Carolina's coast, the way you'd actually meet it — from a car window.

Not the seven natural zones, arranged like an encyclopedia. Two literal road trips, narrated like a documentary tour guide. At every stop, the question is the same: what can you see from the road right here, and what does that tell you about where you are?

Journey 1 · Durham to Nags Head · US-64 East

Watch North Carolina Become Coastal

Begin among the hills and red clay of the Piedmont. Follow the land as it flattens, darkens, floods, opens into sounds, and finally narrows into the Outer Banks.

Someone driving from Raleigh can follow Journey 1 in real time, stop by stop, all the way to the beach. Someone already staying in Nags Head can skip straight to Journey 2 and start reading the islands they're standing on.

Journey 1 teaches how you enter the coastal system — inland Piedmont, through the Coastal Plain, into wetlands, an estuary, two sounds, and finally a barrier island. Journey 2 teaches how to read the coastal system once you're inside it — how the islands themselves vary in width, development, vegetation, exposure, and orientation as you travel their length.

Photography throughout this concept is stand-in stock/placeholder imagery. Real on-location, correct-season photography should replace it before this concept moves forward.
