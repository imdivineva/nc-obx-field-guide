Two logs, one coast

North Carolina's coast, the way you'd actually meet it — from a car window.

Not the seven natural zones, arranged like an encyclopedia. Two mile-by-mile road logs, built to keep open while driving. At every stop: what to look for, what it is, and how long it lasts — log each one as you actually pass it.

Someone driving from Raleigh can follow Journey 1 in real time, stop by stop, all the way to the beach. Someone already staying in Nags Head can skip straight to Journey 2 and start reading the islands they're standing on.

Journey 1 covers how you enter the coastal system — inland Piedmont, through the Coastal Plain, into wetlands, an estuary, two sounds, and finally a barrier island. Journey 2 covers how to read the coastal system once you're inside it — how the islands themselves vary in width, development, vegetation, exposure, and orientation as you travel their length.

Photography throughout this concept is stand-in stock/placeholder imagery. Real on-location, correct-season photography should replace it before this concept moves forward. Logged progress on each journey is saved separately, on your device only.
