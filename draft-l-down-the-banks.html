Beyond the drivable route

Optional extensions

Offered separately rather than folded into the main route — each requires something other than an ordinary car to reach, and each raises complications (beach driving, a second ferry, a boat-only island) that don't belong inside a "northernmost to southernmost point" framing.

Wild Northern Extension

Corolla → Carova

For travelers with the right vehicle, the pavement isn't actually the end of the road. North of Corolla, a four-wheel-drive-only beach route continues to Carova, where wild horses roam among beachfront homes with no paved road at all.

Beyond the Road

Portsmouth Island & Core Banks

South of Ocracoke, the barrier island chain continues — but the road doesn't. Portsmouth Island and Core Banks are reachable only by boat, and represent some of the most remote, undeveloped barrier island in the state.

National Scenic Byway continuation

Ocracoke → Cedar Island

The Outer Banks Scenic Byway doesn't end at Ocracoke. A second ferry continues from Ocracoke to Cedar Island, completing the designated byway back onto the mainland.
